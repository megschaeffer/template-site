Intro

Hello! My name is Meghan, and I am a junior studying Technical Writing and Communication at the University of Minnesota. This is the first time I have ever dealt with HTML and it makes me feel really good about myself. I think things are going fairly well so far, but I am excited to get to know more as I keep using it!

Above is an image of my friends and I at a Gopher football game nearly two years ago now! I definitely miss going to fun events like this before COVID-19.

Work Life

Here is a link to my LinkedIn profile to give you more information about my professional and educational history.

I got my first job at the age of 16 when I applied for a position at Cold Stone Creamery. Since then, I have never been unemployed. Above is a photo of my brother and I in the Cold Stone uniform (though, he only worked that one shift with me after he got special permission from my boss). I worked there for nearly 3 years and was promoted twice during that time. Cold Stone taught me leadership, teamwork, quick problem solving, and multitasking.

For a brief period of time, I worked alongside my mom in the warehouse of Wayzata Bay Spice company. There, we bottled, capped, and labeled bottles of spices. I think this was a good experience to have, but I didn't spend enough time there to develop a close tie with the company.

When I began my freshman year of college, I got a job as a Customer Service Associate (CSA) at a small retail store on campus. Since then, I have also been promoted twice and am now a Senior Student Manager. The retail store is a part of the university's Student Unions and Activities which incorporates several student development outcomes that have really taught me some good career building tactics. This job has taught me to be self-aware, tolerate ambiguity, be responsible and accountable, and much more.

After the fall semester of my Sophomore year, I was asked to be a teaching assistant for a professor within the Carlson School of Management, marking my fourth job. This has been one of the most rewarding work experiences for me as it felt good to be recognized for my hard work in the class and to be able to help others get through it as well.

The most recent job that I have acquired is as a Shift Supervisor at CVS Pharmacy. I originally applied for this job out of fear that my campus retail store would be closed due to COVID-19 (which I ended up being right about). Even though I think of this job as more of a "backup," it has taught me valuable lessons about expectations and procedures in the workplace with its extensive amounts of training, quizzes, and development modules.

My Family

Above is a photo of me and my family at dinner in our favorite vacation spot: Puerto Plata, Dominican Republic.

One of the first things that people know about me is that I have a twin brother. Whenever I am introducing myself to a new group of people, especially new classes, that is always the "fun fact" that I use as an ice breaker. Mat and I both attend the University of Minnesota, and both mistakenly began our college careers in the College of Biological Sciences! Now, he is in the Carlson School and I am in CLA. I love having him so close to me since we get along well and lean on each other often for support.

My mom recently got a job as a receptionist working for Abbott Laboratories, and my dad is a software developer/ data analyst currently working as a consultant for Digi-Key electronics/eXcellentia. He's definitely a good resource to help me develop my digital literacy skills.
